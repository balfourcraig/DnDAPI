Spirit Guardians

  • You call forth spirits to protect you. They flit around you to a distance of 15 feet for the duration. If you are good or neutral, their spectral form appears angelic or fey (your choice). If you are evil, they appear fiendish.
  • When you cast this spell, you can designate any number of creatures you can see to be unaffected by it. An affected creature's speed is halved in the area, and when the creature enters the area for the first time on a turn or starts its turn there, it must make a wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, the creature takes 3d8 radiant damage (if you are good or neutral) or 3d8 necrotic damage (if you are evil). On a successful save, the creature takes half as much damage.

At Higher Levels

When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 4th level or higher, the damage increases by 1d8 for each slot level above 3rd.

General Tips:

  • Use Spirit Guardians when you're surrounded by enemies in combat.
  • Cast the spell when you need to guard a specific area, such as a doorway or a treasure chest.
  • Use Spirit Guardians when you're defending a vulnerable ally, such as a spellcaster who needs to concentrate on a spell.
  • Cast the spell when you want to intimidate enemies and dissuade them from attacking you or your allies.
  • Use Spirit Guardians when you'll be facing undead enemies, as the spell deals extra damage to them.

Interesting Scenarios:

  • Defending a castle or stronghold against attacking enemies
  • Protecting a group of NPCs while they travel through dangerous territory
  • Guarding a sacred or magical location from intruders
  • Assisting in a dungeon crawl by providing area of effect damage to enemies
  • Defending a high-level ritual or spellcasting against interruptions by hostile forces
