Geas

  • You place a magical command on a creature that you can see within range, forcing it to carry out some service or refrain from some action or course of activity as you decide. If the creature can understand you, it must succeed on a wisdom saving throw or become charmed by you for the duration. While the creature is charmed by you, it takes 5d10 psychic damage each time it acts in a manner directly counter to your instructions, but no more than once each day. A creature that can't understand you is unaffected by the spell.
  • You can issue any command you choose, short of an activity that would result in certain death. Should you issue a suicidal command, the spell ends.
  • You can end the spell early by using an action to dismiss it. A remove curse, greater restoration, or wish spell also ends it.

At Higher Levels

When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 7th or 8th level, the duration is 1 year. When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 9th level, the spell lasts until it is ended by one of the spells mentioned above.

General Tips:

  • Use Geas on a powerful enemy to make them do your bidding or leave you alone
  • Use Geas on an NPC to get them to reveal important information or complete a task for you
  • Use Geas on a fellow player character to enforce a promise or important agreement
  • Use Geas on a stubborn ally to convince them to take actions they may not otherwise be willing to take
  • Use Geas to impose consequences on someone who has wronged you or your party

Interesting Scenarios:

  • Forcing a captured enemy to reveal important information about their organization or plans
  • Commanding a sworn enemy to leave the party alone for a set period of time
  • Using Geas as a form of punishment or consequence for NPCs who have betrayed the party or broken a promise
  • Forcing a powerful mage or other entity to aid the party in their quest or battle
