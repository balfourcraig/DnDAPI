Insect Plague

  • Swarming, biting locusts fill a 20-foot-radius sphere centered on a point you choose within range. The sphere spreads around corners. The sphere remains for the duration, and its area is lightly obscured. The sphere's area is difficult terrain.
  • When the area appears, each creature in it must make a constitution saving throw. A creature takes 4d10 piercing damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. A creature must also make this saving throw when it enters the spell's area for the first time on a turn or ends its turn there.

At Higher Levels

When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 6th level or higher, the damage increases by 1d10 for each slot level above 5th.

General Tips:

  • When facing large swarms of insects, such as beetles or spiders.
  • As a distraction to lure enemies away from your party.
  • In situations where a target is vulnerable to insect bites, such as if they're allergic or have an open wound.
  • When navigating through tight spaces or underground caves, where there may be more insects present than usual.
  • As a last resort when facing a particularly difficult enemy, as the insects can distract and weaken them.

Interesting Scenarios:

  • Defending a large city with a swarm of locusts against an enemy army
  • Using the insect plague to disorient and distract a dangerous beast while attempting to capture it
  • Covering a retreat or escape by summoning a horde of biting and stinging insects to slow down pursuers
  • As a distraction during a stealth operation, sending a swarm of insects to cause chaos and distract guards
  • Using the insect plague as a means of torture or intimidation during interrogation

How do you want to do this?:

  • The swarm of insects converges on the target, biting and stinging them until they collapse from the sheer pain.
  • The target attempts to swat at the insects, but only succeeds in injuring themselves as they try to get the bugs off of them.
  • The insects swarm into the target's mouth and nose, suffocating them from the inside as they struggle to breathe.
  • The target becomes covered in so many insects that they are blinded and disoriented, making them an easy target for a final strike.
  • The swarm of insects consumes the target's flesh, leaving only bones behind as they are reduced to a pile of dust.
