Incendiary Cloud

  • A swirling cloud of smoke shot through with white-hot embers appears in a 20-foot-radius sphere centered on a point within range. The cloud spreads around corners and is heavily obscured. It lasts for the duration or until a wind of moderate or greater speed (at least 10 miles per hour) disperses it.
  • When the cloud appears, each creature in it must make a dexterity saving throw. A creature takes 10d8 fire damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. A creature must also make this saving throw when it enters the spell's area for the first time on a turn or ends its turn there.
  • The cloud moves 10 feet directly away from you in a direction that you choose at the start of each of your turns.

At Higher Levels

    • Use the Incendiary Cloud to damage multiple enemies in combat.
    • Use it to create a barrier for protection or to block enemies' sight.
    • Use it to create a diversion or to make an escape from a dangerous situation.
    • Use it to set fire to an area, potentially causing chaos or distracting enemies.
    • Use it to cause environmental damage, such as destroying crops or structures.

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • During a siege, cast the spell on a group of enemy soldiers to create chaos and destruction
    • In a dungeon or cave, use the spell to create a barrier of fire to prevent enemies from following you or to block off a path
    • When ambushed by a group of enemies, use the spell to quickly eliminate multiple targets at once
    • In a forest or grassland, use the spell to create a large fire that clears a path or creates a distraction
    • During a battle on the sea, use the spell to create a fiery barrier between your ship and an enemy ship to prevent boarding or to force them to retreat
