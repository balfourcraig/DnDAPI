Regenerate

  • You touch a creature and stimulate its natural healing ability. The target regains 4d8 + 15 hit points. For the duration of the spell, the target regains 1 hit point at the start of each of its turns (10 hit points each minute).
  • The target's severed body members (fingers, legs, tails, and so on), if any, are restored after 2 minutes. If you have the severed part and hold it to the stump, the spell instantaneously causes the limb to knit to the stump.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • Regenerating health after taking damage in combat
    • Healing party members during a long rest
    • Reviving fallen allies in battle
    • Recovering from exhaustion or fatigue
    • Healing after a particularly difficult or taxing encounter

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • You have just defeated a powerful enemy, but several of your party members are severely wounded. You use the Regenerate spell to heal them quickly so you can continue your adventure without delay.
    • Your rogue has been poisoned and is slowly losing consciousness. You cast Regenerate to cure their poison and bring them back to full health.
    • Your party has been ambushed by a group of trolls. You cast Regenerate to heal the damage they cause and keep your party members alive.
    • Your party has been exploring an ancient tomb and one of your members was cursed with a disease. You use Regenerate to remove the curse and cure the disease before it spreads to other members.
    • One of your party members was hit with a powerful spell that drastically reduced their maximum hit points. You use Regenerate to restore their health to its previous level.
