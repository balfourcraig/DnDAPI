Augury

  • By casting gem-inlaid sticks, rolling dragon bones, laying out ornate cards, or employing some other divining tool, you receive an omen from an otherworldly entity about the results of a specific course of action that you plan to take within the next 30 minutes. The GM chooses from the following possible omens:
  • - Weal, for good results
  • - Woe, for bad results
  • - Weal and woe, for both good and bad results
  • - Nothing, for results that aren't especially good or bad
  • The spell doesn't take into account any possible circumstances that might change the outcome, such as the casting of additional spells or the loss or gain of a companion.
  • If you cast the spell two or more times before completing your next long rest, there is a cumulative 25 percent chance for each casting after the first that you get a random reading. The GM makes this roll in secret.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • Before opening a suspicious-looking chest or door
    • When entering a new room or area for potential traps or dangers
    • Before making a deal with an NPC to determine if they are being truthful
    • When faced with a difficult decision to get a glimpse of possible outcomes
    • Before casting a powerful spell or using a strong magic item to gauge any potential negative consequences
    • When trying to determine the location of a hidden object or individual
    • Before engaging in battle with a powerful opponent to gather information on their strengths and weaknesses
    • When investigating a crime scene to gain insight on the perpetrator

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • Consulting the Augury spell before entering a dangerous dungeon to determine if the party will have a successful outcome or if it would be wiser to turn back.
    • Using the Augury spell before making a difficult moral decision to determine if the outcome would be for the greater good or if it would have negative consequences.
    • Using the Augury spell to determine if an ally or enemy can be trusted before making a deal or going into battle.
    • Consulting the Augury spell before attempting to cast a powerful spell or use a cursed item to determine if the outcome would be favorable or if it would result in negative consequences.
    • Using the Augury spell to determine if a potential lover would bring happiness or heartbreak.
