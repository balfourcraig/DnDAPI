Fear

  • You project a phantasmal image of a creature's worst fears. Each creature in a 30-foot cone must succeed on a wisdom saving throw or drop whatever it is holding and become frightened for the duration.
  • While frightened by this spell, a creature must take the Dash action and move away from you by the safest available route on each of its turns, unless there is nowhere to move. If the creature ends its turn in a location where it doesn't have line of sight to you, the creature can make a wisdom saving throw. On a successful save, the spell ends for that creature.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • When faced with a group of enemies, casting Fear can cause them to flee or become incapacitated, making it easier to take them down.
    • Using Fear on a single powerful enemy can reduce their effectiveness in combat, making them easier to defeat.
    • When trying to intimidate or interrogate an NPC, the Fear spell can be a useful tool to make them more cooperative.
    • In a stealth mission, casting Fear on guards can distract them or cause them to leave their posts, creating an opening for the party to sneak past.
    • When trying to escape from a dangerous situation, casting Fear can cause enemies to hesitate or run away, allowing the party to make a quick getaway.

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • Trick a guard into thinking they are being attacked by a monster, causing them to run away or become incapacitated
    • Intimidate a rival adventurer or antagonist, potentially gaining an advantage in negotiations or combat
    • Scare away a group of bandits or other hostile creatures, allowing safe passage through a dangerous area
    • Create a diversion by causing a group of people or creatures to panic and scatter, allowing the party to slip unnoticed past them
    • Force a captured enemy to reveal valuable information by threatening them with the Fear spell
