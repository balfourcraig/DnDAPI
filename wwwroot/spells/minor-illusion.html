Minor Illusion

  • You create a sound or an image of an object within range that lasts for the duration. The illusion also ends if you dismiss it as an action or cast this spell again.
  • If you create a sound, its volume can range from a whisper to a scream. It can be your voice, someone else's voice, a lion's roar, a beating of drums, or any other sound you choose. The sound continues unabated throughout the duration, or you can make discrete sounds at different times before the spell ends.
  • If you create an image of an object--such as a chair, muddy footprints, or a small chest--it must be no larger than a 5-foot cube. The image can't create sound, light, smell, or any other sensory effect. Physical interaction with the image reveals it to be an illusion, because things can pass through it.
  • If a creature uses its action to examine the sound or image, the creature can determine that it is an illusion with a successful Intelligence (Investigation) check against your spell save DC. If a creature discerns the illusion for what it is, the illusion becomes faint to the creature.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • Use Minor Illusion to create a distraction, allowing your party to make a stealthy escape or to sneak past enemies undetected
    • Create the illusion of a trap or hazard to deter enemies from pursuing you
    • Create an illusory wall or door to conceal your party's movements or to hide valuable items
    • Use Minor Illusion to mimic the voice of an enemy, giving false commands or information to confuse them
    • Create the illusion of a deadly creature or hazard to intimidate enemies and make them hesitate
    • Create an illusory copy of yourself to confuse or distract enemies, while you sneak past undetected
    • Use Minor Illusion to create a sound to lure enemies into a trap or distract them from your party's movements

    • Creating a sound to distract or confuse enemies

    • Creating an illusion of a giant monster to scare off enemies
    • Creating an illusion of treasure to lure enemies into a trap

    • Creating the illusion of a bridge to cross a dangerous gap
    • Creating the illusion of a friendly NPC to negotiate with enemies or distract guards
    • Creating the illusion of a magic item to deceive or distract enemies
