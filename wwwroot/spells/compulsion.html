Compulsion

  • Creatures of your choice that you can see within range and that can hear you must make a wisdom saving throw. A target automatically succeeds on this saving throw if it can't be charmed. On a failed save, a target is affected by this spell. Until the spell ends, you can use a bonus action on each of your turns to designate a direction that is horizontal to you. Each affected target must use as much of its movement as possible to move in that direction on its next turn. It can take any action before it moves. After moving in this way, it can make another Wisdom save to try to end the effect.
  • A target isn't compelled to move into an obviously deadly hazard, such as a fire or a pit, but it will provoke opportunity attacks to move in the designated direction.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • When negotiating with NPCs to convince them to change their mind or behavior
    • In combat to force enemies to attack one another or flee
    • To gather information from NPCs who may be reluctant to share it
    • To prevent NPCs from taking harmful or dangerous actions
    • To compel someone to follow the player's orders or commands

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • Compelling a reluctant witness to testify in court
    • Forcing a captured enemy to divulge important information
    • Commanding a group of enemies to drop their weapons and surrender
    • Convincing a stubborn NPC to provide essential aid or resources to the party
    • Turning an enemy into an ally and recruiting them to help the party
    • Compelling a dangerous animal to retreat or be calm and docile
    • Commanding someone to stand still while the party makes a daring escape
    • Persuading an NPC to release an important hostage or prisoner
    • Compelling a greedy merchant to offer better prices or deals to the party
    • Forcing an enemy to turn on their allies, causing chaos and confusion
