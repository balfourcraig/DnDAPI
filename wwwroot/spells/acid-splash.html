Acid Splash

  • You hurl a bubble of acid. Choose one creature within range, or choose two creatures within range that are within 5 feet of each other. A target must succeed on a dexterity saving throw or take 1d6 acid damage.
  • This spell's damage increases by 1d6 when you reach 5th level (2d6), 11th level (3d6), and 17th level (4d6).

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • Against enemies or creatures that are weak to acid damage.
    • In situations where a loud noise or explosion could be beneficial, as Acid Splash creates a hissing sound upon impact.
    • When trying to ignite or corrode flammable materials or objects.
    • As a surprise attack or in situations where casting a spell without a verbal or somatic component is necessary.
    • When trying to damage multiple enemies at once, as Acid Splash can hit two targets within a 5-foot range of each other.

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • Use Acid Splash to dissolve a trap mechanism that is blocking the group's path.
    • Use Acid Splash to melt the lock on a door to gain access to a treasure room.
    • Use Acid Splash to destroy a monster's weapon or shield, leaving it defenseless.
    • Use Acid Splash to corrode an enemy's armor, weakening their defense.
    • Use Acid Splash to create a diversion by splashing it on a wall, causing a distracting noise and potentially drawing enemies away from the group.

    • Acid melts through armor and sears flesh, leaving a gruesome wound

    • Acidic mist fills the area, dissolving opponents in a cloud of corrosive gas
    • Acid explodes from the caster's fingertips, enveloping the opponent in a cascade of acid
