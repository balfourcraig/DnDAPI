Animal Messenger

  • By means of this spell, you use an animal to deliver a message. Choose a Tiny beast you can see within range, such as a squirrel, a blue jay, or a bat. You specify a location, which you must have visited, and a recipient who matches a general description, such as "a man or woman dressed in the uniform of the town guard" or "a red-haired dwarf wearing a pointed hat." You also speak a message of up to twenty-five words. The target beast travels for the duration of the spell toward the specified location, covering about 50 miles per 24 hours for a flying messenger, or 25 miles for other animals.
  • When the messenger arrives, it delivers your message to the creature that you described, replicating the sound of your voice. The messenger speaks only to a creature matching the description you gave. If the messenger doesn't reach its destination before the spell ends, the message is lost, and the beast makes its way back to where you cast this spell.

At Higher Levels

If you cast this spell using a spell slot of 3nd level or higher, the duration of the spell increases by 48 hours for each slot level above 2nd.

General Tips:

  • Send a warning message to allies about approaching danger from a safe distance
  • Informing allies of possible ambushes or traps in the surrounding area
  • Request for reinforcements or aid from other friendly factions, such as requesting backup from a nearby town or outpost
  • Scouting ahead in dangerous areas or dungeons
  • Delivering messages to NPCs or quest givers who are otherwise difficult to find or communicate with

Interesting Scenarios:

  • As a ranger embarking on a solo mission, sending a message to your party back at camp using a nearby bird or squirrel.
  • As a druid, communicating with a pack of wolves to gather intel on nearby enemies or obstacles.
  • As a rogue, dispatching a friendly rat to stealthily deliver a message to another member of your guild.
  • As a cleric, enlisting the help of a devoted pigeon to deliver a message to a nearby temple or holy site.
  • As a sorcerer, communicating with a flock of seagulls to coordinate a surprise attack on enemy ships.
