Dominate Monster

  • You attempt to beguile a creature that you can see within range. It must succeed on a wisdom saving throw or be charmed by you for the duration. If you or creatures that are friendly to you are fighting it, it has advantage on the saving throw.
  • While the creature is charmed, you have a telepathic link with it as long as the two of you are on the same plane of existence. You can use this telepathic link to issue commands to the creature while you are conscious (no action required), which it does its best to obey. You can specify a simple and general course of action, such as "Attack that creature," "Run over there," or "Fetch that object." If the creature completes the order and doesn't receive further direction from you, it defends and preserves itself to the best of its ability.
  • You can use your action to take total and precise control of the target. Until the end of your next turn, the creature takes only the actions you choose, and doesn't do anything that you don't allow it to do. During this time, you can also cause the creature to use a reaction, but this requires you to use your own reaction as well.
  • Each time the target takes damage, it makes a new wisdom saving throw against the spell. If the saving throw succeeds, the spell ends.

General Tips:

  • When facing a powerful enemy, use Dominate Monster to turn them against their allies or to make them surrender
  • Use Dominate Monster to gain access to restricted areas or information by controlling a creature that can grant you access
  • Use Dominate Monster to complete a difficult quest or task by controlling a creature with the necessary skills or abilities
  • When defending a city or town, use Dominate Monster to take control of attacking monsters and turn the tide of the battle
  • Use Dominate Monster to gain control of a dragon or other powerful creature to use as a mount or ally

Interesting Scenarios:

  • Convincing the enemy boss monster to turn against its own minions, making the battle easier for the party.
  • Using the spell to turn a giant creature, like a dragon, into a mount or ally for the party.
  • Turning a dangerous creature, like a basilisk or medusa, against another enemy, using its petrifying abilities as a weapon.
  • Using Dominate Monster to get valuable information from an enemy, like the location of a hidden treasure or the identity of a mysterious villain.
  • Turning an enemy spellcaster against their own party, causing chaos and confusion on the battlefield.
