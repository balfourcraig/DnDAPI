Seeming

  • This spell allows you to change the appearance of any number of creatures that you can see within range. You give each target you choose a new, illusory appearance. An unwilling target can make a charisma saving throw, and if it succeeds, it is unaffected by this spell.
  • The spell disguises physical appearance as well as clothing, armor, weapons, and equipment. You can make each creature seem 1 foot shorter or taller and appear thin, fat, or in between. You can't change a target's body type, so you must choose a form that has the same basic arrangement of limbs. Otherwise, the extent of the illusion is up to you. The spell lasts for the duration, unless you use your action to dismiss it sooner.
  • The changes wrought by this spell fail to hold up to physical inspection. For example, if you use this spell to add a hat to a creature's outfit, objects pass through the hat, and anyone who touches it would feel nothing or would feel the creature's head and hair. If you use this spell to appear thinner than you are, the hand of someone who reaches out to touch you would bump into you while it was seemingly still in midair.
  • A creature can use its action to inspect a target and make an Intelligence (Investigation) check against your spell save DC. If it succeeds, it becomes aware that the target is disguised.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • Disguising yourself or allies to infiltrate enemy strongholds or gather information.
    • Impersonating a member of a royal or noble family to gain access to restricted areas or people.
    • Mimicking the appearance of a powerful enemy to create a distraction or sow confusion.
    • Concealing your true identity in social situations to avoid suspicion or gain an advantage.
    • Transforming mundane objects into valuable or dangerous items to deceive or intimidate others.

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • Disguising oneself as a member of an enemy faction to gather information or surprise attack them.
    • Pretending to be a wealthy merchant to gain access to exclusive areas or negotiate for better prices.
    • Creating a disguise to infiltrate a group of people to steal an important item or rescue a hostage.
    • Transforming a non-threatening creature or object into a fierce beast to intimidate enemies or distract them from other objectives.
    • Impersonating a powerful ally or leader to rally troops or sway public opinion.
