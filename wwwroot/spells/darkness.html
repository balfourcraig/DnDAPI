Darkness

  • Magical darkness spreads from a point you choose within range to fill a 15-foot-radius sphere for the duration. The darkness spreads around corners. A creature with darkvision can't see through this darkness, and nonmagical light can't illuminate it.
  • If the point you choose is on an object you are holding or one that isn't being worn or carried, the darkness emanates from the object and moves with it. Completely covering the source of the darkness with an opaque object, such as a bowl or a helm, blocks the darkness.
  • If any of this spell's area overlaps with an area of light created by a spell of 2nd level or lower, the spell that created the light is dispelled.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • Use the Darkness spell to temporarily blind enemies and gain the upper hand in combat.
    • Use it to create cover for your party to escape or reposition themselves without being seen.
    • It's also useful for setting traps or hiding objects or people from view.
    • Pair it with spells that allow you to see in the dark, such as Darkvision, to gain an advantage over those who cannot see in the darkness.
    • Use it to obscure dangerous terrain or hazards that others may accidentally stumble into.

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • In a dark dungeon where the party is trying to sneak past some monsters
    • During a nighttime ambush against the party
    • To create cover for a party member to escape unnoticed
    • To hide an important object from enemies
    • To obscure the vision of an enemy spellcaster
