Polymorph

  • This spell transforms a creature that you can see within range into a new form. An unwilling creature must make a wisdom saving throw to avoid the effect. A shapechanger automatically succeeds on this saving throw.
  • The transformation lasts for the duration, or until the target drops to 0 hit points or dies. The new form can be any beast whose challenge rating is equal to or less than the target's (or the target's level, if it doesn't have a challenge rating). The target's game statistics, including mental ability scores, are replaced by the statistics of the chosen beast. It retains its alignment and personality.
  • The target assumes the hit points of its new form. When it reverts to its normal form, the creature returns to the number of hit points it had before it transformed. If it reverts as a result of dropping to 0 hit points, any excess damage carries over to its normal form. As long as the excess damage doesn't reduce the creature's normal form to 0 hit points, it isn't knocked unconscious.
  • The creature is limited in the actions it can perform by the nature of its new form, and it can't speak, cast spells, or take any other action that requires hands or speech.
  • The target's gear melds into the new form. The creature can't activate, use, wield, or otherwise benefit from any of its equipment.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • Use Polymorph to turn yourself or an ally into a creature with useful abilities, such as flight or strong natural weapons.
    • Polymorph can also be used as a form of crowd control, turning enemies into harmless animals or creatures with disadvantage on attacks.
    • If an ally is low on hit points or near death, Polymorph can be used to turn them into a creature with higher hit points to keep them in the battle.
    • Polymorph can also be used for reconnaissance, turning into a small animal to scout ahead or gather information.
    • Finally, Polymorph can be used creatively in puzzles or traps, transforming into the right creature to solve the challenge or bypass the obstacle.

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • Turning into a small rodent to sneak past guards in a dungeon
    • Transforming into a bird to fly up and survey the surrounding area
    • Disguising as a specific individual to gather information or gain access to a restricted area
    • Becoming a larger, stronger creature to battle a powerful enemy
    • Changing into a sea creature to explore underwater ruins or escape pursuers
