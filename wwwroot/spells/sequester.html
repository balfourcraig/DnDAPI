Sequester

  • By means of this spell, a willing creature or an object can be hidden away, safe from detection for the duration. When you cast the spell and touch the target, it becomes invisible and can't be targeted by divination spells or perceived through scrying sensors created by divination spells.
  • If the target is a creature, it falls into a state of suspended animation. Time ceases to flow for it, and it doesn't grow older.
  • You can set a condition for the spell to end early. The condition can be anything you choose, but it must occur or be visible within 1 mile of the target. Examples include "after 1,000 years" or "when the tarrasque awakens." This spell also ends if the target takes any damage.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • Use the Sequester spell to protect an important NPC from harm during a battle or dangerous situation.
    • Sequester a valuable item or artifact to keep it safe from theft or destruction.
    • Hide yourself or a party member from enemies, allowing you to sneak past them undetected.
    • Sequester a party member who has fallen unconscious or is badly injured to keep them safe while the rest of the group fights off enemies.
    • Use Sequester to safely transport a prisoner or captive without risk of them escaping or being freed by others.

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • When you need to trap a powerful enemy or dangerous creature and prevent it from causing harm to your party
    • When you need to temporarily remove an obstacle or barrier that is impeding your progress or exploration
    • When you need to hide an object or artifact from enemies or other individuals who may try to steal it
    • When you need to isolate a group of enemies or civilians in order to prevent them from interfering with your objectives
    • When you need to temporarily remove a creature or character from combat in order to focus on other opponents
