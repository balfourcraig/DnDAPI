Chill Touch

  • You create a ghostly, skeletal hand in the space of a creature within range. Make a ranged spell attack against the creature to assail it with the chill of the grave. On a hit, the target takes 1d8 necrotic damage, and it can't regain hit points until the start of your next turn. Until then, the hand clings to the target.
  • If you hit an undead target, it also has disadvantage on attack rolls against you until the end of your next turn.
  • This spell's damage increases by 1d8 when you reach 5th level (2d8), 11th level (3d8), and 17th level (4d8).

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • Use the Chill Touch spell against undead enemies, as it deals extra damage and prevents them from healing
    • Use Chill Touch against spellcasters who rely on melee attacks, as it can disrupt their spellcasting
    • Chill Touch can be useful in social encounters where you want to intimidate or scare an NPC
    • If you are facing multiple enemies with low health, using Chill Touch can finish them off without wasting higher level spells
    • Chill Touch can be cast as a ranged spell, so it can be useful for taking down flying enemies

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • Defending against undead enemies who are vulnerable to Chill Touch
    • Disrupting the spellcasting of an enemy mage with Chill Touch's ability to prevent them from casting spells
    • Preventing an enemy from healing by using Chill Touch to reduce their maximum hit points
    • Using Chill Touch's ability to deal damage and not leave any physical evidence, useful in stealth missions
    • Preventing an enemy from using a magical item by using Chill Touch's ability to disrupt magical effects
