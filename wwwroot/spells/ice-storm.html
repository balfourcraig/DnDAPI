Ice Storm

  • A hail of rock-hard ice pounds to the ground in a 20-foot-radius, 40-foot-high cylinder centered on a point within range. Each creature in the cylinder must make a dexterity saving throw. A creature takes 2d8 bludgeoning damage and 4d6 cold damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.
  • Hailstones turn the storm's area of effect into difficult terrain until the end of your next turn.

At Higher Levels

When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 5th level or higher, the bludgeoning damage increases by 1d8 for each slot level above 4th.

General Tips:

  • Use Ice Storm to create difficult terrain, hindering enemy movement and giving your allies an advantage in combat.
  • If enemies are clustered together, Ice Storm can deal area damage, hitting multiple targets at once.
  • Ice Storm can also be used as a deterrent, discouraging enemies from approaching or advancing towards your position.
  • Use Ice Storm in combination with other spells or abilities, such as Wall of Ice or Entangle, for even greater crowd control and damage potential.
  • Don't forget that Ice Storm creates actual ice in the affected area, making it a useful tool for exploration and puzzle-solving outside of combat.

Interesting Scenarios:

  • Defending a stronghold against a siege by creating an icy barrier that slows down enemy advance and deals damage to them.
  • Destroying a bridge to prevent enemy troops from crossing a river.
  • Halting a charging foe by freezing the ground in front of them, causing them to slip and fall.
  • Creating a slippery surface on a slope to make it difficult for enemies to pursue you.
  • Cooling down a room or area that has been set ablaze by using the spell to create a localized ice storm.
