Blindness/Deafness

You can blind or deafen a foe. Choose one creature that you can see within range to make a constitution saving throw. If it fails, the target is either blinded or deafened (your choice) for the duration. At the end of each of its turns, the target can make a constitution saving throw. On a success, the spell ends.

At Higher Levels

When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 3rd level or higher, you can target one additional creature for each slot level above 2nd.

General Tips:

  • Use Blindness/Deafness spell to disable spellcasters and limit their ability to cast spells
  • Use it when fighting enemies with low hit points to quickly incapacitate them
  • If a large group of enemies are present, use it to increase your chances of survival and make the fight easier
  • Use Blindness/Deafness spell during stealth missions to eliminate enemies without alerting nearby enemies and causing a commotion
  • If you are attempting to negotiate with a hostile NPC, using Blindness/Deafness spell could be an effective bargaining tool

Interesting Scenarios:

  • Defeating a guard or enemy in a dark area by casting blindness, effectively blinding them and giving the player an advantage
  • Navigating through a maze that has traps triggered by sound, such as a pressure plate that sets off an alarm, by casting deafness
  • Escaping from a dangerous and loud environment, like a dragon's lair, by casting deafness so the player can retreat safely without alerting the enemy
  • Creating a diversion or distraction by casting blindness on a group of enemies, splitting them up and allowing the player to escape or attack from a different angle
  • Helping a fellow party member who has been blinded or deafened by an enemy spell or attack, by casting the opposite spell to remove their impairment
