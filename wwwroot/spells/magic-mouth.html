Magic Mouth

  • You plant a message to an object in the range of the spell. The message is verbalized when the trigger conditions are met. Choose an object that you see, and that is not worn or carried by another creature. Then say the message, which should not exceed 25 words but listening can take up to 10 minutes. Finally, establish the circumstances that trigger the spell to deliver your message.
  • When these conditions are satisfied, a magical mouth appears on the object and it articulates the message imitating your voice, the same tone used during implantation of the message. If the selected object has a mouth or something that approaches such as the mouth of a statue, the magic mouth come alive at this point, giving the illusion that the words come from the mouth of the object.
  • When you cast this spell, you may decide that the spell ends when the message is delivered or it can persist and repeat the message whenever circumstances occur.
  • The triggering circumstance can be as general or as detailed as you like, though it must be based on visual or audible conditions that occur within 30 feet of the object. For example, you could instruct the mouth to speak when any creature moves within 30 feet of the object or when a silver bell rings within 30 feet of it.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • Use Magic Mouth to create diversionary tactics in combat situations, distracting enemies or making them believe reinforcements are coming from a different direction.
    • Create a Magic Mouth trap to protect a valuable item or location, warning intruders or triggering an alarm if they try to break in.
    • Use Magic Mouth to communicate with players who are separated from your character, transmitting messages to them or sharing information about the environment.
    • Create a Magic Mouth spell to confuse or mislead NPCs, allowing you to gain an advantage in negotiations or manipulate them for your own gain.
    • Use Magic Mouth to create a system of signals or codes without the need for spoken language or physical gestures, allowing you to communicate silently and covertly.

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • When negotiating with a difficult NPC, cast Magic Mouth on a nearby object to make it appear as if the NPC is speaking to you and agreeing with your terms.
    • Intrigue scenarios where you cast Magic Mouth on an object in a room to eavesdrop on conversations when you're not physically there.
    • To spook or intimidate enemies, cast Magic Mouth on a skull or other macabre object to make it appear as if it's speaking a warning or curse.
    • In a puzzle or trap-heavy dungeon, use Magic Mouth to create clues or hints for navigating the space.
    • In a social setting, cast Magic Mouth on a fellow party member to boost their charisma or persuasion abilities during conversations.
