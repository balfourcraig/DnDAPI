Clairvoyance

  • You create an invisible sensor within range in a location familiar to you (a place you have visited or seen before) or in an obvious location that is unfamiliar to you (such as behind a door, around a corner, or in a grove of trees). The sensor remains in place for the duration, and it can't be attacked or otherwise interacted with.
  • When you cast the spell, you choose seeing or hearing. You can use the chosen sense through the sensor as if you were in its space. As your action, you can switch between seeing and hearing.
  • A creature that can see the sensor (such as a creature benefiting from see invisibility or truesight) sees a luminous, intangible orb about the size of your fist.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • When exploring new areas, use Clairvoyance to scout ahead and gather information without putting the party in danger.
    • Use Clairvoyance to spy on enemies or gather intel on their plans.
    • When navigating a labyrinth or maze, use Clairvoyance to see which path is the correct one.
    • Use Clairvoyance to search for hidden treasures or secret rooms.
    • When attempting to rescue hostages, use Clairvoyance to locate where they are being held.

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • Scouting ahead to find hidden traps or secrets
    • Gaining insight into an opponent's battle strategy
    • Searching for an important object or person in a large area
    • Checking if a room is occupied before entering
    • Observing an ongoing conversation from a distance
    • Detecting the presence of invisible creatures or illusions
    • Getting a glimpse of future events or potential outcomes

    • Checking if an NPC is lying or telling the truth
