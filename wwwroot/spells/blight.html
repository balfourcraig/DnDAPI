Blight

  • Necromantic energy washes over a creature of your choice that you can see within range, draining moisture and vitality from it. The target must make a constitution saving throw. The target takes 8d8 necrotic damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. The spell has no effect on undead or constructs.
  • If you target a plant creature or a magical plant, it makes the saving throw with disadvantage, and the spell deals maximum damage to it.
  • If you target a nonmagical plant that isn't a creature, such as a tree or shrub, it doesn't make a saving throw; it simply withers and dies.

At Higher Levels

When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 5th level of higher, the damage increases by 1d8 for each slot level above 4th.

General Tips:

  • In combat, use Blight to inflict heavy damage on enemy spellcasters or boss-type monsters.
  • When navigating through areas with lots of foliage or plant life, Blight can be used to clear a path quickly.
  • If your party is low on rations or food, use Blight to destroy plants and gain sustenance.
  • When dealing with enemy factions that rely on agriculture or farming, using Blight to ruin their crops can weaken their position and give your party an advantage.
  • Blight can be used to create distractions by causing sudden plant deaths, diverting attention away from your party.
  • In creative roleplaying situations, Blight can be used for artistic or aesthetic purposes, such as sculpting objects out of dead plant matter.
