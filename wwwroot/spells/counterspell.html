Counterspell

You attempt to interrupt a creature in the process of casting a spell. If the creature is casting a spell of 3rd level or lower, its spell fails and has no effect. If it is casting a spell of 4th level or higher, make an ability check using your spellcasting ability. The DC equals 10 + the spell's level. On a success, the creature's spell fails and has no effect.

At Higher Levels

When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 4th level or higher, the interrupted spell has no effect if its level is less than or equal to the level of the spell slot you used.

General Tips:

  • Use Counterspell when facing a spell caster enemy to prevent them from casting powerful spells that could harm your party
  • Consider using Counterspell to interrupt a spell that is targeting an ally, such as a healing spell cast by an enemy cleric
  • When facing a boss monster, save your Counterspell for their most dangerous spells to prevent catastrophic damage to your party
  • Pay attention to enemy spellcasters' verbal and somatic components to know when they are casting a spell that can be countered
  • Consider investing in the Counterspell spell as a sorcerer or wizard to have it always prepared and available when needed

Interesting Scenarios:

  • Stopping an enemy spellcaster from using a powerful spell, potentially saving your party from certain defeat.
  • Preventing a magical trap from triggering, avoiding damage or other harmful effects.
  • Disrupting a spell that is being cast on one of your allies, protecting them from the spell's negative effects.
  • Counterspelling an illusion spell that is trying to deceive you or your party.
  • Interrupting a ritual being performed by an enemy group, potentially preventing them from summoning a dangerous creature or completing a nefarious plan.
