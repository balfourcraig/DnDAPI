Project Image

  • You create an illusory copy of yourself that lasts for the duration. The copy can appear at any location within range that you have seen before, regardless of intervening obstacles. The illusion looks and sounds like you but is intangible. If the illusion takes any damage, it disappears, and the spell ends.
  • You can use your action to move this illusion up to twice your speed, and make it gesture, speak, and behave in whatever way you choose. It mimics your mannerisms perfectly.
  • You can see through its eyes and hear through its ears as if you were in its space. On your turn as a bonus action, you can switch from using its senses to using your own, or back again. While you are using its senses, you are blinded and deafened in regard to your own surroundings.
  • Physical interaction with the image reveals it to be an illusion, because things can pass through it. A creature that uses its action to examine the image can determine that it is an illusion with a successful Intelligence (Investigation) check against your spell save DC. If a creature discerns the illusion for what it is, the creature can see through the image, and any noise it makes sounds hollow to the creature.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • Create an illusion of a large, intimidating creature to frighten enemies
    • Project an image of a peaceful scene to distract and pacify NPCs
    • Use the spell to create a decoy of yourself to evade capture or ambush
    • Create an image of an NPC or creature to negotiate with or deceive others
    • Use the spell to create a visual representation of complex plans or strategies for your party

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • When infiltrating an enemy stronghold, use the Project Image spell to distract guards while your allies sneak in undetected.
    • Use the spell to create a decoy of yourself during a high-risk mission, making it more difficult for enemies to track down and attack the real you.
    • When negotiating with a powerful NPC, use the spell to present a convincing illusion of the NPC's greatest ambitions, swaying their opinion in your favor.
    • Use Project Image to create a convincing illusion of an ancient artifact or relic, distracting enemies or convincing them to give up their own search for the real thing.
    • When attempting to gather information in secret, use the spell to arouse less suspicion as you pose as another character or disguise yourself as someone else entirely.
