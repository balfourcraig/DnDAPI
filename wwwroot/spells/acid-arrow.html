Acid Arrow

A shimmering green arrow streaks toward a target within range and bursts in a spray of acid. Make a ranged spell attack against the target. On a hit, the target takes 4d4 acid damage immediately and 2d4 acid damage at the end of its next turn. On a miss, the arrow splashes the target with acid for half as much of the initial damage and no damage at the end of its next turn.

At Higher Levels

When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 3rd level or higher, the damage (both initial and later) increases by 1d4 for each slot level above 2nd.

General Tips:

  • Use the Acid Arrow spell to deal damage to enemies from a distance
  • Use it to target enemies behind cover or barriers
  • Use it to finish off a wounded enemy before they can escape
  • Use it to weaken or destroy structures or objects

Interesting Scenarios:

  • Defeating a group of enemies that are resistant to physical attacks
  • Creating a diversion by targeting a flammable object with the acid arrow
  • Breaking down a door or barrier with the corrosive properties of the acid arrow
  • Forcing an enemy to retreat by causing damage over time with the persistent acid damage
  • Using the spell as a ranged option when unable to close the distance to enemies

How do you want to do this?:

  • Acid Arrow pierces through the enemy's chest, leaving a burning hole and melting internal organs.
  • The Acid Arrow strikes the enemy's head, causing their skull to dissolve and a sickening gurgling sound to escape their mouth.
  • As the Acid Arrow hits the enemy's stomach, their body convulses as acid eats away at their insides, leaving them writhing in agony.
  • The Acid Arrow slams into the enemy's back, melting through armor and bone before dissolving their spine and causing them to collapse in a paralyzed heap.
  • As the Acid Arrow lands on the enemy's leg, it eats through flesh and bone, causing them to stumble and collapse in excruciating pain.
