Dream

  • This spell shapes a creature's dreams. Choose a creature known to you as the target of this spell. The target must be on the same plane of existence as you. Creatures that don't sleep, such as elves, can't be contacted by this spell. You, or a willing creature you touch, enters a trance state, acting as a messenger.
  • While in the trance, the messenger is aware of his or her surroundings, but can't take actions or move.
  • If the target is asleep, the messenger appears in the target's dreams and can converse with the target as long as it remains asleep, through the duration of the spell. The messenger can also shape the environment of the dream, creating landscapes, objects, and other images. The messenger can emerge from the trance at any time, ending the effect of the spell early. The target recalls the dream perfectly upon waking. If the target is awake when you cast the spell, the messenger knows it, and can either end the trance (and the spell) or wait for the target to fall asleep, at which point the messenger appears in the target's dreams.
  • You can make the messenger appear monstrous and terrifying to the target. If you do, the messenger can deliver a message of no more than ten words and then the target must make a wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, echoes of the phantasmal monstrosity spawn a nightmare that lasts the duration of the target's sleep and prevents the target from gaining any benefit from that rest. In addition, when the target wakes up, it takes 3d6 psychic damage.
  • If you have a body part, lock of hair, clipping from a nail, or similar portion of the target's body, the target makes its saving throw with disadvantage.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • When trying to gain information or insights from the subconscious mind of a sleeping NPC or enemy
    • To communicate with a distant ally or party member through shared dreams
    • To learn more about the history or lore of a particular location or item by accessing dream memories
    • To gain creative inspiration or ideas for solving a puzzle or obstacle in the game
    • To plant suggestions or ideas in the dreams of an NPC or enemy, influencing their actions or decisions
    • To gain temporary reductions in exhaustion or mental fatigue by experiencing restful dreams

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • Entering a dangerous dream realm to retrieve vital information from a sleeping enemy
    • Communicating with a lost ally or loved one through their dreams to gather information about their whereabouts
    • Manipulating the dreams of a powerful NPC to gain leverage or control over them
    • Entering the dreams of a group of NPC allies to gather clues or insight into a mysterious situation
    • Using Dream to infiltrate the dreams of a villain and attempt to turn them to your side or gather information about their plans

    How do you want to do this?:

    • Dream Walk: The target falls asleep and immediately enters a dream state, where they can be manipulated by the caster.
    • Nightmare: The target experiences a terrifying nightmare that causes physical and psychological damage.
    • Dreamweaver's Curse: The target becomes cursed with bad dreams, causing them to suffer disadvantage on all attack rolls and ability checks until the curse is lifted.
    • Mind Scramble: The target's mind is confused and disoriented, causing them to lose their next turn in combat.
    • Sleep Paralysis: The target is unable to move or take actions for one turn, leaving them vulnerable to attacks.
