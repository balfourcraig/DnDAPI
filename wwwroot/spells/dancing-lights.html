Dancing Lights

  • You create up to four torch-sized lights within range, making them appear as torches, lanterns, or glowing orbs that hover in the air for the duration. You can also combine the four lights into one glowing vaguely humanoid form of Medium size. Whichever form you choose, each light sheds dim light in a 10-foot radius.
  • As a bonus action on your turn, you can move the lights up to 60 feet to a new spot within range. A light must be within 20 feet of another light created by this spell, and a light winks out if it exceeds the spell's range.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • Use Dancing Lights to create distraction and confuse enemies during combat.
    • Create a path of light to navigate through dark caves or dungeons.
    • Send one of the lights ahead to scout for traps or hidden enemies.
    • Create a signal system with the lights to communicate with other party members in the dark.
    • Use the lights as a makeshift torch to light up a dark room or area.

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • Creating a distraction during a heist or burglary by having the lights dance in one area while the player sneaks in unnoticed
    • Using the lights to intimidate or scare enemies during a battle
    • Casting the spell to impress or entertain NPCs during social interactions
    • Guiding a group through a dark dungeon or cave system when the player doesn't have dark vision
    • Casting the lights on a specific location to draw attention to a hidden object or clue
