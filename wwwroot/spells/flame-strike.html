Flame Strike

A vertical column of divine fire roars down from the heavens in a location you specify. Each creature in a 10-foot-radius, 40-foot-high cylinder centered on a point within range must make a dexterity saving throw. A creature takes 4d6 fire damage and 4d6 radiant damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.

At Higher Levels

When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 6th level or higher, the fire damage or the radiant damage (your choice) increases by 1d6 for each slot level above 5th.

General Tips:

  • Use Flame Strike when fighting against multiple enemies
  • Use it in tight spaces to maximize damage
  • When facing enemies with high armor class or resistance to physical damage
  • If you know the enemy is likely to be clustered together
  • To clear out obstacles or environmental hazards

Interesting Scenarios:

  • Defending a castle from a siege.
  • Clearing out a cavern filled with hostile creatures.
  • Stopping a group of bandits raiding a trade caravan.
  • Battling a powerful dragon or other legendary creature.
  • As a distraction to cause chaos in a crowded marketplace.

How do you want to do this?:

  • Conjures a fiery explosion, reducing enemies to ash

  • Blasts a single target with intense heat, leaving only charred remains
  • Ignites the air itself, burning enemies alive in a sea of fire
  • Engulfs a group of foes in a massive inferno, leaving nothing but scorched earth
