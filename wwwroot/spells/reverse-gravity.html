Reverse Gravity

  • This spell reverses gravity in a 50-foot-radius, 100-foot high cylinder centered on a point within range. All creatures and objects that aren't somehow anchored to the ground in the area fall upward and reach the top of the area when you cast this spell. A creature can make a dexterity saving throw to grab onto a fixed object it can reach, thus avoiding the fall.
  • If some solid object (such as a ceiling) is encountered in this fall, falling objects and creatures strike it just as they would during a normal downward fall. If an object or creature reaches the top of the area without striking anything, it remains there, oscillating slightly, for the duration.
  • At the end of the duration, affected objects and creatures fall back down.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • Use the Reverse Gravity spell to lift enemies off their feet and suspend them upside down.
    • Make it harder for enemies to reach you by reversing the gravity in a chamber or hallway.
    • Create a trap by reversing gravity in a room with a trapdoor or pit, causing creatures to fall up instead of down.
    • Escape dangerous situations by reversing gravity and floating to safety.
    • Use the spell defensively by reversing gravity when falling from a high height so that you fall more slowly and can safely land.

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • Flipping the gravity in a room to surprise and defeat enemies attacking from different angles.
    • Using Reverse Gravity to escape from a pit or climb out of a ravine by walking on the ceiling.
    • Using the spell to set a trap for enemies by creating an area of reversed gravity that causes them to float helplessly.
    • Creating a aerial marketplace by reversing gravity in an open space where people can sell wares while suspended in mid-air.
    • Using Reverse Gravity to create a temporary bridge across a gap or chasm that players can cross while walking on the ceiling.
