Control Weather

  • You take control of the weather within 5 miles of you for the duration. You must be outdoors to cast this spell. Moving to a place where you don't have a clear path to the sky ends the spell early.
  • When you cast the spell, you change the current weather conditions, which are determined by the GM based on the climate and season. You can change precipitation, temperature, and wind. It takes 1d4 x 10 minutes for the new conditions to take effect. Once they do so, you can change the conditions again. When the spell ends, the weather gradually returns to normal.
  • When you change the weather conditions, find a current condition on the following tables and change its stage by one, up or down. When changing the wind, you can change its direction.
  • ##### Precipitation
  • | Stage | Condition |
  • |---|---|
  • | 1 | Clear |
  • | 2 | Light clouds |
  • | 3 | Overcast or ground fog |
  • | 4 | Rain, hail, or snow |
  • | 5 | Torrential rain, driving hail, or blizzard |
  • ##### Temperature
  • | Stage | Condition |
  • |---|---|
  • | 1 | Unbearable heat |
  • | 2 | Hot |
  • | 3 | Warm |
  • | 4 | Cool |
  • | 5 | Cold |
  • | 6 | Arctic cold |
  • ##### Wind
  • | Stage | Condition |
  • |---|---|
  • | 1 | Calm |
  • | 2 | Moderate wind |
  • | 3 | Strong wind |
  • | 4 | Gale |
  • | 5 | Storm |

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • When you need to create a distraction to sneak past guards or enemies, use Control Weather to conjure up a sudden and violent rainstorm or thunderstorm.
    • If you want to quickly extinguish a raging forest fire, use Control Weather to summon a rainstorm over the affected area.
    • To gain the upper hand in a battle, use Control Weather to summon a strong wind or blizzard to hinder your enemies' movement and visibility.
    • When traveling long distances, use Control Weather to ensure clear skies and gentle winds, making the journey more comfortable and efficient.
    • If you need to navigate dangerous waterways, use Control Weather to calm the seas or create a fog to obscure yourself from enemy ships.

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • Halting a severe thunderstorm while traversing a dangerous mountain pass.
    • Calming a raging hurricane to allow for safe passage across the ocean.
    • Summoning a fog bank to obscure your movement and ambush enemies on a battlefield.
    • Creating a rainstorm to extinguish deadly fires or to aid farmers during a drought.
    • Conjuring a snowstorm to slow down pursuing enemies or to create a frozen bridge over a body of water.
