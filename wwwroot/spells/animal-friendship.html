Animal Friendship

This spell lets you convince a beast that you mean it no harm. Choose a beast that you can see within range. It must see and hear you. If the beast's Intelligence is 4 or higher, the spell fails. Otherwise, the beast must succeed on a wisdom saving throw or be charmed by you for the spell's duration. If you or one of your companions harms the target, the spells ends.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • Use the Animal Friendship spell to calm down hostile or frightened animals and prevent them from attacking you or your party members. This can be especially useful when navigating through forests, swamps, or other wilderness areas where dangerous animals may be present.
    • Cast the spell to befriend non-threatening animals or to gain their trust. This can be useful for gathering information, tracking prey, or navigating through unfamiliar terrain by following animal trails.
    • Use Animal Friendship to distract or lure away animals that are blocking your path or preventing you from accessing a particular area.
    • When interacting with sentient species that have animal companions, use the spell to gain the trust and approval of the creature. This can help build a positive relationship with the creature's owner or tribe.
    • Consider using Animal Friendship as a way to pacify or control large groups of animals, such as herds of elk or packs of wolves. This can be especially useful when attempting to pass through an area without causing harm or when hunting for food.

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • You need to cross a river, but the only way to do so is by riding on the back of a crocodile. You use Animal Friendship to convince it to let you hitch a ride.
    • You need to infiltrate a fortress guarded by vicious guard dogs. Instead of fighting them, you use Animal Friendship to gain their trust and sneak past them.
    • You come across a pack of wild wolves. Instead of attacking them, you use Animal Friendship to establish a peaceful relationship and potentially recruit them as companions.
    • You need to get information from a wise old owl. You use Animal Friendship to gain its trust and convince it to share its knowledge with you.
