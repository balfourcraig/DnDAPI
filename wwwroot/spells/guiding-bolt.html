Guiding Bolt

A flash of light streaks toward a creature of your choice within range. Make a ranged spell attack against the target. On a hit, the target takes 4d6 radiant damage, and the next attack roll made against this target before the end of your next turn has advantage, thanks to the mystical dim light glittering on the target until then.

At Higher Levels

When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 2nd level or higher, the damage increases by 1d6 for each slot level above 1st.

General Tips:

  • Use Guiding Bolt when you need to deal a large amount of damage to a single target.
  • It can be particularly effective against enemies with high AC or resistance to physical damage.
  • If your party is struggling to take down a tough enemy, Guiding Bolt can give everyone an advantage on their attacks against it.
  • If you're playing as a cleric, Guiding Bolt can be a great early-game spell to help deal damage while conserving your other spell slots.
  • If you're playing as a multiclassed character, Guiding Bolt can be a good option to have as a ranged attack when your other abilities are more suited to close combat.

Interesting Scenarios:

  • In a boss battle against a powerful enemy, to deal a significant amount of damage and hopefully turn the tide of the fight.
  • To quickly take out a weaker enemy who might be a threat to the party, such as an archer or spellcaster.
  • As a distraction or diversion, casting the spell in a different area to draw enemy attention away from the party's main objective.
  • To create a glowing beacon or signal in the distance, helping the party find their way in the dark or alerting allies to your location.
  • In a tense negotiation or conversation with an NPC, casting the spell as a show of force or influence to create a stronger bargaining position.
