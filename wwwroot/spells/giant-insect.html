Giant Insect

  • You transform up to ten centipedes, three spiders, five wasps, or one scorpion within range into giant versions of their natural forms for the duration. A centipede becomes a giant centipede, a spider becomes a giant spider, a wasp becomes a giant wasp, and a scorpion becomes a giant scorpion.
  • Each creature obeys your verbal commands, and in combat, they act on your turn each round. The GM has the statistics for these creatures and resolves their actions and movement.
  • A creature remains in its giant size for the duration, until it drops to 0 hit points, or until you use an action to dismiss the effect on it.
  • The GM might allow you to choose different targets. For example, if you transform a bee, its giant version might have the same statistics as a giant wasp.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • Use the Giant Insect spell to temporarily gain a powerful ally in battle, such as a giant spider or centipede.
    • Send the giant insects to scout ahead and search for hidden traps or enemies.
    • Use the giant insects to distract enemies while you sneak past them unnoticed.
    • Have the giant insects climb walls or ceilings to create a surprise attack from above.
    • Use the giant insects to carry small objects or messages to hard-to-reach places.

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • As a distraction: Cast the Giant Insect spell to summon a swarm of wasps, bees or ants to distract an enemy while you make your escape.
    • As a scout: Send a giant beetle or spider ahead of your party to explore a dangerous area, collecting information and reporting back.
    • As a mount: Ride a giant flying insect like a giant bee or dragonfly into battle or to reach a hard-to-reach location.
    • As a trap: Use the Giant Insect spell to lure an enemy into a trap, such as a pit filled with a swarm of angry hornets.
    • As a weapon: Summon a swarm of angry wasps or a giant centipede to attack enemies and deal damage to them.
