Eyebite

  • For the spell's duration, your eyes become an inky void imbued with dread power. One creature of your choice within 60 feet of you that you can see must succeed on a wisdom saving throw or be affected by one of the following effects of your choice for the duration. On each of your turns until the spell ends, you can use your action to target another creature but can't target a creature again if it has succeeded on a saving throw against this casting of eyebite.
  • ***Asleep.*** The target falls unconscious. It wakes up if it takes any damage or if another creature uses its action to shake the sleeper awake.
  • ***Panicked.*** The target is frightened of you. On each of its turns, the frightened creature must take the Dash action and move away from you by the safest and shortest available route, unless there is nowhere to move. If the target moves to a place at least 60 feet away from you where it can no longer see you, this effect ends.
  • ***Sickened.*** The target has disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks. At the end of each of its turns, it can make another wisdom saving throw. If it succeeds, the effect ends.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • Use Eyebite on powerful enemies to stun or put them to sleep, giving your party an advantage in combat.
    • Use it in social situations to intimidate someone or persuade them to do what you want.
    • Use it to temporarily incapacitate a guard or other obstacle while your party sneaks past.
    • Use it to protect yourself or an ally from a dangerous enemy by causing them to be frightened.
    • Use it to escape a dangerous situation by causing a pursuer to fall asleep or be frightened.

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • Convincing a guard to let you pass without showing your identification
    • Scaring away an enemy in combat by causing them to feel a sense of terror
    • Seducing a NPC to get valuable information or items
    • Causing an ally to become immune to fear effects before a dangerous battle
    • Forcing a target to fall asleep at an opportune moment
