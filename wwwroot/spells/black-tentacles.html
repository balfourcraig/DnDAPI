Black Tentacles

  • Squirming, ebony tentacles fill a 20-foot square on ground that you can see within range. For the duration, these tentacles turn the ground in the area into difficult terrain.
  • When a creature enters the affected area for the first time on a turn or starts its turn there, the creature must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw or take 3d6 bludgeoning damage and be restrained by the tentacles until the spell ends. A creature that starts its turn in the area and is already restrained by the tentacles takes 3d6 bludgeoning damage.
  • A creature restrained by the tentacles can use its action to make a Strength or Dexterity check (its choice) against your spell save DC. On a success, it frees itself.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • During combat with a group of enemies to restrict their movement and reduce their attacks
    • While defending a chokepoint or narrow passage to prevent enemies from advancing
    • To trap or restrain a fleeing opponent
    • As a distraction to draw attention away from a more fragile ally or to create an opportunity for sneaking or flanking
    • When exploring a dungeon or tight space to clear out potential threats without expending too much energy or resources

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • To ensnare a group of enemies for easier combat
    • To create a distraction and allow the player or party to sneak past guards or obstacles
    • To block off an exit or bottleneck to prevent enemies from escaping or entering
    • To grab a key or important item held by an enemy and drag it towards the player
    • To create a barrier around allies during a battle, providing both protection and an offensive advantage

    How do you want to do this?:

    • The tentacles reach up from the ground and wrap tightly around the opponent, crushing them to death
    • The tentacles drag the opponent down into a portal to the Shadowfell, effectively removing them from combat
    • The tentacles wrap around the opponent's limbs and tear them apart, dealing massive damage and potentially causing limb loss
    • The tentacles constrict around the opponent's head, suffocating them to death
    • The tentacles drag the opponent into the air and slam them into the ground, dealing a devastating amount of bludgeoning damage
