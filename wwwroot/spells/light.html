Light

  • You touch one object that is no larger than 10 feet in any dimension. Until the spell ends, the object sheds bright light in a 20-foot radius and dim light for an additional 20 feet. The light can be colored as you like. Completely covering the object with something opaque blocks the light. The spell ends if you cast it again or dismiss it as an action.
  • If you target an object held or worn by a hostile creature, that creature must succeed on a dexterity saving throw to avoid the spell.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • Light up dark areas - this is especially useful for exploration and finding important items or clues.
    • Disrupt enemy tactics - casting Light on an enemy's weapon or shield can temporarily blind them, giving your allies an advantage.
    • Create a distraction - casting Light in a different area from where you plan to attack can draw enemies away from your true target.
    • Mark your path - leaving a trail of Light can help you and your party find your way back through a maze-like dungeon or other complex environment.
    • Create a signal - casting Light in a specific pattern or location can alert allies to your presence, or convey a pre-arranged message.

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • Illuminating a dark cavern or dungeon
    • Creating a distraction by shining bright light on a hidden enemy
    • Blinding opponents in combat by casting the spell directly in their eyes
    • Guiding a group through a dangerous forest at night
    • Signaling for help by flashing the light spell
    • Examining intricate details of an object or artifact in dim light
    • Scaring off creatures who fear bright light
