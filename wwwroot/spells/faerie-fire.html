Faerie Fire

  • Each object in a 20-foot cube within range is outlined in blue, green, or violet light (your choice). Any creature in the area when the spell is cast is also outlined in light if it fails a dexterity saving throw. For the duration, objects and affected creatures shed dim light in a 10-foot radius.
  • Any attack roll against an affected creature or object has advantage if the attacker can see it, and the affected creature or object can't benefit from being invisible.

At Higher Levels

    General Tips:

    • Use Faerie Fire to reveal invisible enemies or objects.
    • Cast Faerie Fire on enemies in combat to grant advantage on attack rolls against them.
    • Use Faerie Fire to mark targets for other spellcasters, making it easier for them to hit with spells that require an attack roll.
    • Cast Faerie Fire on an area to reveal hidden traps or secrets.
    • Use Faerie Fire on enemies who rely on stealth or camouflage to make them easier to track or spot.

    Interesting Scenarios:

    • During a stealth mission, cast Faerie Fire on an enemy guard to alert your allies of their location without making noise.
    • In a chaotic battle with many enemies, cast Faerie Fire on them to mark them for your allies to target.
    • Exploring a dark dungeon, cast Faerie Fire to light up a hidden room or reveal hidden enemies.
    • During a chase scene, cast Faerie Fire on the fleeing target to slow them down or reveal their location to your allies.
    • In a roleplaying scenario, use Faerie Fire as a visual effect to enhance your storytelling or intimidate an NPC.
